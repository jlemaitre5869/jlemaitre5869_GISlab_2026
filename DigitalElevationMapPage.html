Digital Elevation Model Analysis

This purpose of this project is to create a Digital Elevation Model Map. I have decided to compare an old map and my own digital elevation map to see how humans have interacted and manipulated the land over time. My region of interests is the port of Rotterdam along the Maas River. I chose this region because I wanted to learn how the port development has impacted the natural landscape over time.

Project Overview

My research objective, for this project, is to determine how the development of the port of Rotterdam, over time, has affect the natural landscape, specifically the Maas River. I will be doing this by creating a DEM map and comparing it to an old map. The old map I have chosen for this project is the Biesbosch, made in 1858, see map gallery below. The map was made by W.C.H. Staring in the Geoplaza institution. The publisher is Haarlem: A.C. Kruseman and it was commissioned by Topographic arrangement of The Hague; Topographic Bureau of War. I felt that QGIS was an appropriate method to use because it allows me to produce a DEM map, which I can use to analysis and compare the natural landscape with the old map. I felt this has both environmental and social relevance because it shows us the impact we have on our environment through the development of our society.

Methods

To produce the DEM map I will be using QGIS to produce both the DTM and DSM of the Rotterdam Port. I will then use the georeferencing tool on QGIS to align the old map with my DEM map.

  • Data collection = QGIS’ Quick OSM plugin and Website: Old Map Online
  • Cartographic design choices = These choices will include; what colour to use to show the difference in elevation, what scale/range to use for the DSM and DTM to show the difference in elevation and how to overlay the old map to be able to compare the landscape.
  • Software and tools used = QGIS

Results & Findings

The comparison between the maps showed that the old map had more islands present in the river. But now in the new map due to port industrialisation the islands have merged. The DEM map also showed that there are more ports or docking areas found along the river. We can also see in the map that there is more elevation on the islands and in the cities due to the buildings. The pattern of the DEM map shows that as you go away from the river the elevation decreases. We can also see some dykes present on the land near the river. There is also a spike outside of the city, north of the river, which could possibly be an old landfill or dumbing site. But by comparing these two maps we can see how much impact our society is having on our natural landscape.

Reflection

Through this project I learnt how to make a DEM map, through downloading the DSM and DTM of an area. I learnt how to overlay layers on top of one another. Moreover, I learnt how to align two maps together by using georeferencing points. Finally, I learnt how to clip your maps that you’ve created. This project allowed me to understand the multiple steps which are required to produce something like a DEM map.

The project limitation was despite using the georeferencing points, the map still was not exactly aligned. Therefore, future improvements could be to use multiple maps from the year 1858 and compare how the port changed from all of the maps. I also initially chose a map which would not align at all with the DEM. This was probably because the old map had not been drawn accurately. Thus, a lesson I learned was to try other maps and that it is sometimes ok to give up on a map if its not working. Moreover, I had problems with rendering the DTM and DSM maps as my areas was too big. So an important lesson I learnt is that it is better to use smaller areas.
